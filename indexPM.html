My PM-38 Boat Build

This was supposed to be my summer of 2024 project. Unfortunately, I couldn't finish the project in 2 months, and I've been slow in finishing it since the school year started. Now I am almost finished and only need to waterproof all the wood and add fiberglass to the hull.

It is a wooden boat 13.5' long. I got the plans for this boat from a popular mechanics article from 1962. All the wood was from a nearby lumberyard and Home Depot. The boat will be powered by a 1988 Johnson 28 SPL. I am also a high school student, with this being my first experience building something this large. It is far from perfect but is good enough for me.

First Steps

Frame No. 4 and the Transom

July 9, 2024

I finished frame No. 4 and the transom. They were pretty easy to make and looked good. I had to make and use some gussets for frame No. 4 because I didn't think the wood overlapped enough for the lap joint to be as strong as I liked. Everything was fastened together using primarily 1 inch #8 stainless steel Philips screws and epoxy.

Completion of the Skeleton

I made all the parts needed to make the Boat Skeleton

July 19, 2024

All frames/parts needed to make the shape of the boat were done. I had made frame No. 2, stem, and keelson. The next step was to assemble everything.

Assembling Parts

First look at the boat

July 26, 2024

I assembled all the parts I had to make the initial shape of the boat. I had to assemble 2 A-frames to prop up the boat. Frames No. 2 and 4 were not glued/screwed onto the keelson yet and were only hanging on with clamps.

Battens and Chine logs

The Boat takes more shape!

Augy 1, 2024

Frame No. 2 and 4 were attached to the keelson. I also cut the battens and chine logs which were screwed onto the frames+transom.

Bottom Planking

Adding plywood to the boat

Augy 12, 2024

I connected the chine logs to the stem and did the bottom planking. The planking was the most fun part as it was pretty easy and satisfying.

Rest of the Bottom Planking

Doing the hard part of the bottom planking

Augy 18, 2024

The front planking was difficult because of the curve. I messed up cutting out the plywood on my first try, making a piece way too small even though I was overcutting from my measurements. I had to steam the plywood to make it bend without cracking.

Side Planking

Giving the boat some walls!

Sepy 10, 2024

Doing the side planking for the front of the boat was a breeze but the back portion was difficult. I needed extra hands so I used my car jack to prop the plywood up so I could screw it into the chine log. After doing both left and right sides I added the sheer rails (a long thin piece of wood at the bottom of the side planking in image 3)

Errors in Symmetry

Fixing mistakes I made with the sheer rails

Octy 2, 2024

I had messed up when doing the sheer rails. One side was much higher than the other and it looked weird. I had cut the side planking to the edge of the sheer rails thinking that both rails would bend the same way. But they hadn't and I only realized that after flipping the boat over. So I decided to just cut off the weird plywood section and add new plywood there, then trace the correct shape and add a new portion of spray rail onto the part where I cut off. (screws sticking out were temporary. I later pulled them out after gluing all the plywood/lap joints together. using 1 inch screws for 2 overlapping 1/4" pieces of plywood is pretty dumb but if it worked I wasn't going to question it)

Engine

Buying and testing my outboard

Octy 6, 2024

I bought my outboard (1988 Johnson 28 SPL) from Facebook marketplace. I did a compression check on each cylinder and they were both fine. But I made a mistake while wiring up the battery to the solenoid where I connected both the positive and negative to the solenoid on each side of it (image 3) because the previous owner labeled a + and - onto it which I misinterpreted. This resulted in a horrible arcing noise and started welding the positive lead onto the battery. I tried finding the right wiring scheme by just looking at the components. I looked at the starter and saw how there was only 1 wire leading into it which I thought was very strange. I researched and saw how the engine block was the ground for the starter, so I only connected the battery's positive to the solenoid and the negative to the block. Then my engine was able to turn over. I later was able to get it running but did not get it on video.

Fixed Sheer Rail

Finished up fixing my mistake

Octy 7, 2024

I finally glued the missing portion of the sheer rail back and trimmed away the excess plywood. I also added on the middle deck batten.

Deck Battens

Adding the 2 side deck battens

Octy 15, 2024

I cut and screwed the 2 side deck battens on. This step was quite bad, as I couldn't get the wood to bend enough before cracking. I wasted 3 battens like that even though I tried steaming them. One did work, so I got the least cracked batten and added another piece of wood underneath the cracked portion to strengthen it and add it on. That curve was a lot stiffer than the other side but It's not the worst part of this boat.

Deck

Making and adding the deck

Novy 15, 2024

I cut out the deck and trimmed it slightly. This step was not the hardest nor easiest. The only annoying part was finding plywood I could use as I didn't store the remaining plywood I had left properly so some were water-damaged. My images show the deck after I unscrewed it off. I also waterproofed the inside of the back section of my boat at some point with a layer of epoxy which can be seen in the third picture (shininess).

Rain and Mold

Doing damage control

Presenty , 2024

Due to me not finishing sooner this boat now has to endure the rainy season. This caused a lot of mold on the inside of the boat which I hadn't coated with epoxy. At first, I sanded the areas with mold but this became too tedious. I then turned to chemical solutions using vinegar at first, but that did not kill all of it and it grew back. So I used bleach that was diluted and mixed with TSP which seems to have worked so far. The images show the boat being treated with that mixture. Now the boat is just covered with a tarp because of the rain.
